🧠 The intelligence ladder · how the brains get smarter over time
L1
Follows the rules
Shows up on schedule, does what it's told, reports back. Reliable — but not smart. Same output, every time.
L2
Tracks its own score
Grades itself after every call. Win, lose, or miss — it logs it honestly and keeps a public track record to prove it.
L3
Must earn the right to trade
No strategy goes live without passing a test on real historical data first. The gauntlet catches losers before they cost real money.
L4
Writes its own playbook
Invents new trading signals from scratch, stress-tests each one, and only keeps the survivors. No human writes the rules — it does.
L5 ✦
Learns how to learn
Notices which types of approach keep winning — then steers its own invention engine toward more of those. Not just learning from trades. Learning from its own learning.

Jarvis Forex
EUR/USD · GBP/USD · USD/JPY · AUD/USD · Session-aware
L5
"The same signal performs differently in London open vs NY close — so the brain learns by session, not just by strategy."
What it does
Scalps the four major FX pairs across active trading sessions. It knows the difference between London breakout, NY momentum, and Asian consolidation — and adjusts position sizing and signal filters accordingly. Session-awareness is built into the strategy tagging, not bolted on.
The AI layer
Claude Opus weekly reflection — reviews session-by-session family performance, invents new strategy variants tuned to specific sessions and pair characteristics, then validates each through a spread + swap cost-aware gauntlet on 5-minute historical bars. Losses in one session window don't contaminate another.
Self-improvement engine
L5: A strategy that works in London breakout but fails in NY consolidation is labeled and scored independently per session. Family insights (breakout, carry, mean-reversion, momentum) are tracked per pair and per session window — the invention engine steers toward combinations that have proven durable.

Jarvis Scout
Autonomous strategy factory · Discovers → Validates → Deploys → Retires
L9
"It doesn't just run strategies — it writes them, tries to kill them, turns survivors into live bots, then retires them when they stop working."
What it does
A fully autonomous strategy factory. It discovers strategy hypotheses, runs each through a 7-gate gauntlet + adversarial red-team, generates a complete trading bot for survivors, incubates it in paper trading, monitors for performance decay, and retires it when the edge disappears — all without human intervention.
The AI layer
Claude Sonnet 4.6 (three LaunchAgents, 24/7). Each cycle: a Discovery agent brainstorms hypotheses from market regimes and historical patterns. A Validation agent runs the gauntlet. A Generation agent writes the full bot code. A Monitoring agent tracks live performance and calls retirement when Sharpe degrades. Execution via OKX and Futu.
What L9 means
L9 is the highest level in the fleet. Beyond L5 (family-level learning), L9 closes the full loop: invent → validate → deploy → monitor → retire → learn why. Every retired bot's failure reason feeds back into the discovery engine — making future hypotheses smarter. The system has never needed a human to suggest an idea.
